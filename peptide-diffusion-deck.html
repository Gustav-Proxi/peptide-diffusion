The Problem
De Novo Sequencing

A spectrometer shatters proteins.
We read the pieces.

Mass spectra are sparse, noisy fingerprints — a ladder of fragment-ion masses that encode the original amino-acid sequence.

Existing tools depend on a known reference proteome. For wastewater, novel pathogens, or environmental samples, that database doesn't exist.

Classical autoregressive baselines (CasaNovo, InstaNovo) plateau at 33% peptide accuracy.

02 / 13
Our Approach
Three Contributions

Diffusion, not autoregression.
Bidirectional context wins.

i.

Absorbing diffusion
over amino-acid tokens

Every position starts as [MASK]. The model iteratively reveals positions in any order, using bidirectional context — left and right neighbors at every step.

x0 ← fθ(xt, S)
ii.

Three targeted
architectural contributions

PeakEncoder — learned attention over raw m/z peaks. B/Y-ion bias — physics of fragment-ion masses. Self-conditioning — the biggest single architectural win.

+14.6 pp from self-conditioning alone
iii.

Iterative inference
with confidence ranking

CFID — eight-candidate mask-predict refinement. SGIR — score candidates by how well predicted fragments match observed peaks. Free at inference, no retraining.

CFID + SGIR → 76.00% AA · 59.96% Pep

Pipeline · End-to-End

Three contributions on top of absorbing diffusion.

05 / 13
Contribution · The biggest single win
Self-Conditioning

Let the model see
its own previous answer.

Standard diffusion sees only the noisy state xt.

Self-conditioned diffusion also sees the model's own previous estimate x̂0prev.

A one-line change to the forward pass.
2× the compute per step.
+14.6 percentage points on peptide accuracy.

Trained with stop-gradient on the previous estimate — pure inference-time memory.

06 / 13
Inference-time refinement
CFID · Confidence-Filtered Iterative Decoding

Eight candidates, mask-predict
refinement, top-k commit.

Don't commit positions all at once. Draw 8 candidate sequences. Lock in the most confident positions across candidates. Resample the rest. Repeat.

Effectively an ensemble inside a single model — no retraining, no extra parameters.

Self-cond argmax: 57.84% Pep
Self-cond + CFID: 59.60% Pep

+1.76 pp peptide accuracy

07 / 13
Final ranking
SGIR · Self-Guided Iterative Ranking

Re-score by spectrum match.
Free win.

CFID gives 8 candidate sequences. Compute the predicted fragment-ion masses for each. Score against the actual observed peaks.

Pick the candidate whose predicted spectrum matches the observed spectrum best.

CFID alone: 59.60% Pep
CFID + SGIR: 59.96% Pep ✦

+0.36 pp · stable across all 3 seeds

Reproducibility · 9 failure modes
Negative Results

Nine failures, fully reproduced
across three seeds.

①

Mass correction post-hoc

50 ppm precursor-mass filter discards near-correct candidates faster than wrong ones.

−9.6 pp vs CFID+SGIR
②

Mass-corr only (Option B)

Without CFID safety net: even worse than #1.

−10.0 pp vs argmax
③

Mass-constrained beam search

Left-to-right autoregressive decoding is architecturally incompatible with bidirectional diffusion.

−44.9 pp · catastrophic
④

Entropy-adaptive gate alone

Aggressively skips low-confidence positions. Kills accuracy entirely (12.00% AA).

−48.1 pp · catastrophic
⑤

Rerank-spectral as standalone

Pure spectral-match rescoring (no CFID).

−6.3 pp
⑥–⑨

Other documented failures

Gate+CFID+SGIR (statistically identical to CFID+SGIR · no benefit), removing PeakEncoder, linear noise schedule, beam+SGIR (does not rescue beam).

all reproduced · 3 seeds
