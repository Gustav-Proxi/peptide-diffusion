THE PROBLEM

A spectrometer shatters proteins.
We read the pieces.

Mass spectra are sparse, noisy fingerprints — a ladder of fragment-ion masses that encode the original amino-acid sequence. Recovering that sequence without consulting a reference database is the central problem.

When you're sequencing a mixed microbial community from wastewater, you don't have a reference proteome. De novo sequencing lets you recover peptide identities even from organisms that don't exist in any known database.

01 · The Spectrometer

Trypsin digests, MS2 shatters. Proteins are cut into peptides, then fragmented along their backbone. The break produces complementary b-ions (N-terminal) and y-ions (C-terminal).

02 · The Wall

Database search fails when the proteome is unknown. For wastewater, gut microbiomes, and novel pathogens, that database doesn't exist. E. coli: 92%. Wastewater: 14%. Novel organisms: 3% coverage.

03 · Our Approach

Treat sequencing as absorbing multinomial diffusion: start fully masked, refine all positions simultaneously, condition on the spectrum and the previous step's estimate.

Absorbing diffusion is native-discrete: it corrupts tokens by replacing them with [MASK] and learns to unmask them — no quantization error. At T=500, every position is masked. The model refines all positions simultaneously with full bidirectional attention.

ARCHITECTURE

Three targeted contributions
on top of absorbing diffusion.

Each contribution is independently ablated. Together they take the model from 41.88% AA recall (plain absorbing diffusion) to 76.00%, and from 5.44% to 59.96% peptide accuracy.

① PeakEncoder

Sinusoidal m/z encoding + log-intensity projection. AlphaFold3-inspired learnable scalar bias on complementary b/y ion pairs. +33 pp AA · physics-informed but safe.

② Self-Conditioning

T=500 cosine-schedule steps. At each step, a preview pass produces a coarse estimate fed back as additional context. +14.6 pp peptide acc · biggest single gain.

③ CFID + SGIR Reranking

Beam search K=8 with knapsack mass filter. Rerank by cosine similarity of theoretical vs. observed fragment ion distributions. Pareto-optimal · best pep acc, lower variance.

Attention Mask Fix

Cross-spectrum contamination in batches — V1 batches leaked information across spectra. Diagonal attention mask fix recovered ~2 pp AA without architectural change.

Key Lessons

1. PeakEncoder is non-negotiable — without it, diffusion = GRU.
2. Self-conditioning is the biggest single gain (+14.6 pp).
3. Inference constraints must match generative direction — bidirectional models cannot be decoded left-to-right.
